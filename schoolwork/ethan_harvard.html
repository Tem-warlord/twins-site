Some fun facts about harvard

    Harvard holds real estate totalling 5,457 acres of land, the size of over 4000 football fields! But it hasn’t always been this large. Classes at Harvard began in the summer of 1638 in a single frame house and a college yard. Of the total land owned, the main university campus holds only about 4%. With all that land, there has got to be a couple of libraries, right? Harvard has some of the world’s most famous university libraries that boasts a collection that is the oldest in America. There are 79 libraries, the main and largest one is known as the Widener Memorial Library. It houses about 3.5 million works in over 100 languages, and 92 kilometres of shelves across ten levels.
