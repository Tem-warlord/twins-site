Facts about WSU

  1. WSU delivered $406.7 million in financial aid and scholarships for undergraduate students (2018-19). In the 2018-19 academic year, 76% of WSU undergraduates received some form of financial aid.
  2. WSU went though several name changes. From Weber State Academy to Weber Normal College. Yes, you read that right, normal. I had a hard time believing that It had the word normal in the name, but it was typical for teacher for colleges at the time.
  3. WSU's original name was Washington State Agricultural College and School of Sciences. It is located in Richland, Washington.
  4. The mascot, Butch T. Cougar, was first seen in 1927.
  5. In 1993, WSU Vancouver was one of the first institutions in the country to use interactive electronic classrooms. (classes via video)

    What you need to know to get in

    1. You need a GPA of 3.4 so you should mostly get A's and B's.
    2. You'll want a SAT score of 1000 or better, you should aim higher.
    3. Washington state doesn't have a minimum ACT requirement, but if you apply with a 20 or below you'll have a tough time getting in.
